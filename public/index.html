Bonjour, France!

From the romantic streets of Paris to the sun-drenched vineyards of Bordeaux, my trip through France was everything I had dreamed of and more. Here's a look at the places I visited, the food I savored, and the memories I made.

Places I Visited

🗼

Paris

The City of Light never disappoints. I climbed the Eiffel Tower at sunset, wandered through the Louvre, and strolled along the Seine. Every corner felt like a painting come to life.

🏰

Loire Valley

A fairy-tale landscape dotted with grand chateaux. Visiting Chambord and Chenonceau felt like stepping back into the Renaissance, surrounded by immaculate gardens and history.

🍷

Bordeaux

Wine country at its finest. I toured renowned vineyards, tasted world-class Merlots and Cabernets, and enjoyed fresh oysters from Arcachon Bay just an hour away.

🏖️

French Riviera

Nice, Cannes, and Monaco — the Riviera is pure glamour. Crystal-clear Mediterranean waters, vibrant markets, and gorgeous coastal scenery made this the perfect final stop.

⛰️

Mont Saint-Michel

An island abbey rising dramatically from the sea — truly one of the most breathtaking sights in all of France. Visiting at high tide felt almost otherworldly.

🥮

Lyon

Considered the gastronomic capital of France, Lyon's traditional bouchons served up unforgettable quenelles, saucisson, and tarte aux pralines. A must for any food lover.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

April–June and September–October offer mild weather and fewer crowds than peak summer.

Getting Around

The TGV high-speed rail connects major cities quickly. In Paris, the Metro is fast and affordable.

Language

Learning a few basic French phrases goes a long way. Locals appreciate the effort, even a simple "Bonjour!"

Food & Dining

Lunch is often the biggest meal. Look for the "plat du jour" (dish of the day) for great value at local bistros.

Book Ahead

Reserve tickets for the Eiffel Tower, Louvre, and popular chateaux in advance to skip long queues.

Currency

France uses the Euro. Credit cards are widely accepted, but keep some cash for markets and small cafes.
